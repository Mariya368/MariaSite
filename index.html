I live in Kharkiv.
It is the second-largest city in Ukraine and has a population of 1.5 million people.
Kharkiv was the first capital of the Ukrainian, from December 1919 to January 1934, after that the capital relocated to Kiev.
There are 6 museums, 7 theatres and 80 libraries. Presently, Kharkiv is a major cultural, scientific, educational, transport and industrial centre of Ukraine.
Kharkiv is the largest city at 50 parallel. Kharkiv is not considered the largest city in Ukraine and neighboring countries, but if you draw an invisible line of 50 parallels, then it is really the largest.
Kharkiv is an international student city. Due to the large number of universities with directions for foreign students, from September to June in the streets you can meet people from all over Africa, Asia, Europe and Latin America.
For the first time the atomic nucleus was cleaved in Kharkov. A group of scientists led by Georgy Latyshev split the nuclear core in Kharkov for the first time. This event took place in 1932 at the Physicotechnical Institute.
Depth of Pushkinskaya metro station. Metro station "Pushkinskaya" on the Saltovskaya branch has a depth of 10-storey house, that is, 35 meters. The Pushkin station was built in 1984, it is decorated with reliefs and compositions from the life of the famous Russian poet Alexander Pushkin. This is the deepest station of the Kharkiv Metro.
